MUSICAL INSTRUMENTS

Music is an art form that combines either vocal or instrumental sounds, sometimes both, using form, harmony, and expression of emotion to convey an idea.

Musical Instruments are classified into Five different categories depending on the manner in which the instruments creates the sound.They are:


1.Idiophones

2.Membranophones

3.Chordophones

4.Aerophones

5.Electrophones

1.Idiophones

Idiophones are instruments whose own substance vibrates to produce sound


Examples:
  • Bells
  • Clappers

    • 2.Membranophones

    Membranophones emit sound by the vibration of a stretched membrane


    Examples:
  • Drums
  • Tambourines

    • 3.Chordophones

    Chordophones are a class of musical instruments in which a stretched, vibrating string produces the initial sound


    Examples:
  • Violin
  • guitar

    • 4.Aerophones

    An aerophone is a musical instrument that produces sound primarily by causing a body of air to vibrate, without the use of strings or membranes


    The basic types include woodwind, brass, and free-reed instruments
  • Woodwind

    • Woodwind instruments are a family of musical instruments within the greater category of wind instruments


    Examples:
  • Clarinet
  • Flute
  • Basson

  • Brass instrument

    • A Brass instrument is a musical instrument that produces sound by sympathetic vibration of air in a tubular resontar in sympathy with the vibration of the player's lips


    Examples:
  • French horn
  • Tuba
  • trombone

  • Free-reed instruments

    • A free reed aerophone is a musical instrument that produces sound as air flows past a vibrating reed in a frame.


    Examples:
  • Harmonicas
  • Accordions

    • 5.Electophones

    Electrophone is a musical instruments in which the initial sound either is produced by electronic means.


    Examples:
  • Digital piano
  • Elecronic keyboard
  • Electronic organ
